<h2 id="where">Where your work actually lives</h2>

- **On your device.** Every keystroke lands in a local database on your device (SQLite — on desktop, a plain file in the app's data folder). No round-trip, no server copy. You can export everything as plain markdown at any time from **Settings → Data**.
- **Between your devices.** When you share a space, documents travel directly between member devices over an encrypted peer-to-peer channel. The keys are derived on your devices from the invite you created; no server ever holds them.
- **Through a relay — only when needed.** When a direct connection can't be made, traffic is encrypted on-device with AES-256-GCM and passes through a relay that cannot decrypt it and keeps no record of it.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Don't trust the page — read the code.">Every claim here is enforced by source you can audit. The encryption, the relay, and the protocol are all in the [repository](https://github.com/hamza512b/tasfer) — the apps are AGPL-3.0, the editor libraries MIT. Fork it, swap the relay, or ship your own build — the refusal is in the code, and the code is yours to keep.</Callout>

<h2 id="keys">Your keys</h2>

Your identity is a keypair generated on your device the first time the app runs. It is stored in the app's local database, its private half is never transmitted, and it is never escrowed with anyone. There is no key-recovery service — which also means there is no master key for anyone to compel. Your devices hold the only copies of your work, so treat the markdown export in **Settings → Data** as your backup: if you lose every device, there is no server to recover from.
