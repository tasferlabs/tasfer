<p className="dx-lede">Nothing syncs until you share it. When you share a space, your devices talk **directly, peer-to-peer over WebRTC, with keys that never leave your devices.** A signaling relay exists only to introduce devices that can't see each other directly — and it can't read a thing.</p>

<h2 id="how">How sync works</h2>

Three stages, in plain terms:

- **Introduce.** A signaling relay tells your two devices how to reach each other. It holds no accounts and learns nothing else.
- **Direct.** Your devices negotiate an encrypted peer-to-peer WebRTC channel and talk to each other with no third party in the path.
- **Fallback.** If a direct path can't be made (strict NAT, locked-down network), traffic is encrypted on-device with AES-256-GCM and forwarded through the relay as bytes it cannot decrypt. Still no accounts, still no logs.

<h2 id="share">Share a space</h2>

Sync is per-space: a space syncs only with the devices you invite into it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create an invite">
    In the sidebar, open the space menu and choose **Invite members**. Tasfer generates an invite containing a discovery topic and a secret, shown as an **Invite Code** and a **QR Code**. The secret is created locally and is never sent to the relay.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join from the other device">
    On the second device choose **Join someone's space** — paste the code, scan the QR, or import an invite file. The devices exchange public keys directly, prove they hold the invite secret, and derive a shared encryption key. Your identity keypair is generated locally and its private key never leaves the device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit anywhere">
    The space now converges across every member device — edit offline on one and the changes merge cleanly when the devices next see each other.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="warn" title="Treat invite codes like passwords.">Anyone who has the code can join the space, and codes do not currently expire. Share them over a channel you trust, and prefer the QR code when the other device is in the same room.</Callout>

<h2 id="default-relay">The default relay</h2>

Out of the box, Tasfer uses `wss://signaling.cypher.md` for the introduce/fallback step. It holds no accounts, stores no messages, and is structurally unable to decrypt your documents. If that's not enough trust — and it shouldn't have to be — run your own.

<h2 id="own-relay">Use your own relay</h2>

The relay endpoint is fixed when the app is built, via the `VITE_SIGNAL_URL` environment variable:

```bash
# in apps/web, before building or running the app:
VITE_SIGNAL_URL=wss://signaling.example.org npm run build
```

There is currently no in-app setting to change it at runtime — pointing at your own relay means building the app yourself.

<Callout kind="tip" title="Your relay, your rules.">Running your own relay means no third party — not even the Tasfer project — is ever in the path of an introduction. See [Self-hosting the relay](/docs/app/self-hosting).</Callout>
