---

Peer-to-peer apps and networks have advantages and disadvantages, like everything in life. Respecting privacy is a big win for many: no one in the world can decide to share your content except you. You are in full control.

But it also poses risks. What if a malicious actor tries to take advantage of this to share malicious content? To be clear, no one can stop them.

This is really the age-old question of what is good and what is bad. Who am I to limit people's access and add gates? And why should people trust a company either?

In my view, we all bear the responsibility to make sure such things not happen - every stakeholder, not only the developers. Whatever safeguards we set, people will find a way around them.
